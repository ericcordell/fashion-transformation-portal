📄 Overview Guide

Each program card has an Overview tab that tells the story of why an initiative exists, what it does, and what value it delivers. This guide helps you understand what each section means — and how to make yours as clear and compelling as possible.

What is the 💡 i writing tip badge?

You may notice a small grey i circle next to some section labels. Clicking it opens a panel with suggestions for how to strengthen that section's content. It's a quiet coaching nudge — it only appears when there's something worth saying, and it never scores or grades your work.

🗺️ How Overview fields map to your OPIF

The three sections in the Overview tab pull directly from fields in

Portal Section OPIF / Jira Field
🎯 Problem Statement Problem Statement field in your OPIF
📄 Description Description field in your OPIF
📈 Business Impact Business Impact / Business Benefit field in your OPIF

✨ AI-assisted content: If any of these fields are empty in your OPIF, the portal will automatically use AI to generate a draft based on all other available content in the epic — including the title, labels, and context. You can always improve on it by updating the field directly in Jira.

🎯 Problem Statement

This section answers: Why does this initiative exist? Who is hurting, and how?

What makes it strong

  • Named persona — who specifically feels this pain? (e.g. Fashion Merchants, Buying Associates, Planning Teams)
  • Current-state friction — what do they do today that is slow, error-prone, or frustrating?
  • Stakes — what breaks downstream, or what opportunity is being missed?
  • Leads with pain, not with the solution

Example: "Today, Fashion Merchants spend 3+ hours per week manually reconciling buy plan data across spreadsheets, OPIF, and BPE — resulting in version control errors and delayed supplier commitments."

📄 Description

This section answers: What are we building, and how does it solve the problem?

What makes it strong

  • Names the system or capability — what specifically is being built? (e.g. an AEX-native workflow, a BigQuery pipeline, a Centric integration)
  • Describes the HOW — active verbs: "will enable", "automates", "integrates", "surfaces"
  • Connects to the beneficiary — so [persona] can [outcome] instead of [current pain]

Example: "AEX will surface a unified item setup workflow that automatically pulls attributes from OneSource, eliminating manual re-entry — so Merchants can focus on strategy rather than data reconciliation."

📈 Business Impact

This section answers: Why does this matter to the business — and by how much?

What makes it strong

  • A number — even directional ($50MM GMV, 2,000 hours/year, 15% error reduction, 3 FTE equivalent)
  • The narrative — how does solving this problem create that value? Connect the dots.
  • Both together is what makes a business case stick in leadership reviews

Example: "Estimated $30MM in incremental GMV through faster, more accurate assortment decisions — plus 1,800 hours of Merchant time recovered annually by eliminating manual reconciliation work."

✅ Requirements for Portal Inclusion

Primary Requirement: LLTT or LLTTO Label

Your OPIF MUST include either the label LLTT or LLTTO to appear in this portal.

These are the two accepted labels that determine whether an OPIF is tracked in the E2E Fashion Portal. Use LLTT for standard long-lead-time initiatives or LLTTO for operational long-lead-time items.

How to Add the Label:

  1. Open your OPIF in Jira (jira.walmart.com)
  2. Look for the "Labels" field (usually in the right sidebar)
  3. Click "+ Add" or click on the Labels field
  4. Type LLTT or LLTTO and press Enter
  5. Save the OPIF

🔄 Workstream Assignment Logic

The workstream displayed in the portal (Strategy, Design, Buying, or Allocation) is auto-populated — you do not need to set it manually.

🧠 How Workstreams Are Assigned:

The portal analyzes the entire OPIF content — including the epic name, description, personas, labels, and overall context — and automatically assigns the most appropriate workstream using predefined business rules. The richer your OPIF content, the more accurately the portal can place it.

Workstream Categories:

📊 Strategy

Strategic initiatives, planning, forecasting, and transformation programs that span multiple workstreams

🎨 Design

Product design, UX/UI, PLM (Centric), conceptualization, and creative development activities

🛒 Buying

Procurement, vendor management, buy planning (BPE/DBP), assortment planning, and purchasing

📦 Allocation

Distribution, inventory management, allocation planning (AEX), and fulfillment optimization

📌 If the workstream looks wrong:

Because assignment is automatic, occasional mismatches can happen — especially for cross-functional initiatives. If your OPIF is appearing in the wrong workstream, reach out to the E2E Fashion Portal support team (see the "Need Help?" section) and they can correct it directly.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Common issues and solutions:

Q: My OPIF isn't showing up in the portal

A: Check the following:

  1. Verify the LLTT or LLTTO label is added to your OPIF
  2. Wait 24 hours for the next automated sync
  3. Ensure your OPIF is in an active status (not Closed/Done)
  4. Check that your OPIF appears on the Confluence dashboard

Q: My status/date is outdated in the portal

A: The portal syncs daily from Confluence. Updates made in Jira will appear in the portal within 24 hours after the next automated sync runs (typically early morning).

Q: Which field should I update to change my status?

A: Update BOTH fields:

  • Status Color (Green/Yellow/Red) — visible in the OPIF sidebar
  • Ready to Start Field in the top header (Ready to Start, In Progress, Blocked, etc.)

Q: My Assignee and Product Manager don't match

A: They should be the same person. Update the Assignee field to match your Product Manager for consistency. The portal uses the Assignee field as the primary owner.

Q: How do I change which workstream my OPIF appears in?

A: Workstream assignment is automatic — the portal infers it from your OPIF content and personas. If your OPIF is appearing in the wrong workstream, contact the E2E Fashion Portal support team (see "Need Help?") and they can correct it directly.

🔄 Sync Schedule

Understanding when your updates will appear in the portal:

📅

Daily Automated Sync

The portal automatically syncs with Confluence every business day (Monday - Friday).

⏰ Changes you make in Jira today will appear in the portal the next business day morning.

📌 Weekend updates will appear on Monday morning.

⚡

Data Flow

Jira OPIF → Confluence Dashboard → E2E Fashion Portal

📌 Make sure your OPIF is visible on the Confluence dashboard to ensure it syncs to the portal.
